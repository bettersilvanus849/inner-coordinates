inner-coordinates helps you run sequences of clicks and actions on your computer. You teach the software the location of buttons and menus once. Then, the tool remembers these positions to perform complex tasks for you. This handles applications that hide their internal data from normal automation tools. 

## 🛠️ How this software helps

Many programs do not share their layout with your computer. This makes it hard to build automated workflows. inner-coordinates bypasses these blocks by using visual maps. You look at your application, tell the software where items live, and save that map. You can then trigger these steps later. This saves time on tasks you repeat every day.

## 🚀 Setting up your first map

1. Open inner-coordinates from your desktop icons.
2. Click the Capture button in the top menu.
3. Move your mouse cursor over a button in the app you want to automate.
4. Click once to record the coordinate point.
5. Name the point in the text box that appears.
6. Press Save to store the location in your workspace.
7. Repeat this for every button or menu you need to touch.

## ⚙️ Running a workflow

Once you save your points, you build a workflow. A workflow is just a list of steps in a specific order.

1. Select the Workflows tab in the main window.
2. Click Create New. 
3. Drag your saved coordinate points into the main area.
4. Arrange them in the sequence you want the app to click.
5. Set the delay between clicks if the target app needs time to load.
6. Click Run to start the sequence.

## 💡 Troubleshooting common issues

If the software misses a click, your screen resolution might have changed. Relaunch your target application and ensure the window size matches its size during the initial capture. Some apps block inputs, so run inner-coordinates as an administrator if your clicks do not register. 

Contact the support team if the software crashes during long loops. Ensure you do not overlap windows while the tool runs, as it tracks pixels based on their position on your screen. Keep your target application on the primary monitor for best results. If you use multiple screens, keep the target app on the one used during the training phase.

## 🛡️ Privacy and data

Your coordinate maps stay on your computer. We do not store your private data or screen captures on external servers. The software processes all inputs locally to ensure your tasks remain private and secure. You control access to your applications at all times. Turn off the software when you want to stop all automation. 

## 📝 Best practices for success

Start with simple tasks like opening a menu and clicking a single button. As you gain skill, build longer sequences that span across different menus. Test each step individually before you chain them together into a long workflow. Use the pause feature to check the progress between clicks. This ensures the computer keeps pace with the software you control. Label your files clearly to keep your workspace organized. Delete old maps that you no longer use to maintain speed.
